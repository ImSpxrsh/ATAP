# References / key literature

Verified during the project (searches logged in `outputs/logs/priorart_log.md`). Fill exact
volume/page and confirm each citation against the source before submission.

## ATAP peptide (the agent)
1. **De G, Ko J-K, Tan T, Zhu H, Li H, Ma J.** Amphipathic tail-anchoring peptide is a promising
   therapeutic agent for prostate cancer treatment. ***Oncotarget*** 2014;5(16):7734–47. PMC4202157.
   *(ATAP-iRGD-M8; sequences in Table 2; prostate xenografts.)*
2. **Ko J-K, Choi K-H, Pan Z, et al.** The tail-anchoring domain of Bfl1 and HCCS1 targets
   mitochondrial membrane permeability to induce apoptosis. ***J Cell Sci*** 2007;120(16):2912–23.
   *(Original ATAP / Bfl-1 tail anchor, BAX/BAK-independent MOMP.)*
3. **Ko J-K, et al.** Amphipathic tail-anchoring peptide and BH3 peptides induce apoptosis through
   different mechanisms. 2011 (PMC3059050/3059055). *(ATAP acts independently of Bcl-2-family status.)*

## Executioner (BAX/BAK) loss & venetoclax resistance — the target population
4. **Moujalled DM, Pomilio G, Ghiurau C, … Wei AH.** Acquired mutations in BAX confer resistance to
   BH3-mimetic therapy in acute myeloid leukemia. ***Blood*** 2023;141(6):634–644. PMC10651776.
   *(~17% of AML acquire inactivating BAX mutations at venetoclax relapse — the executioner-loss population.)*
5. Commentary: Acquired BAX mutations in AML. ***Blood*** 2023;141(6):562.
6. **Combined BCL-2 + MCL-1 inhibition overcomes BAX-deficiency-mediated resistance in TP53-mutant
   AML.** ***Blood Cancer J*** 2023 (PMC10123065). *(BAX deficiency → single-agent BH3-mimetic resistance.)*

## Functional apoptotic priming (the decisive readout we need)
7. **Vo TT, Ryan J, … Letai A.** Reduced mitochondrial apoptotic priming drives resistance to BH3
   mimetics in acute myeloid leukemia. ***Cancer Cell*** 2020 (PMC7988687). *(BH3 profiling; priming
   loss drives venetoclax + S63845 resistance.)*
8. **Minihane E-J, … Ryan J, Neuberg DS, Letai A, Garcia JS.** Dynamic BH3 profiling predicts
   clinical outcomes in acute myeloid leukemia. ***bioRxiv*** 2025, doi:10.1101/2025.10.14.682414.
   PMID 41278625. *(Prospective DBP, 92 AML patients; functional priming + venetoclax delta-priming.)*
9. **Montero J, Letai A.** Why do BCL-2 inhibitors work and where should we use them? (BH3 profiling
   framework.) *(General reference for dynamic BH3 profiling.)*

## Venetoclax resistance mechanisms / confounders

    independently recovered by our discrimination battery, ρ=+0.721.)*
11. **Nechiporuk T, … Tyner JW.** The TP53 apoptotic network is a primary mediator of resistance to
    BCL2 inhibition in AML. ***Cancer Discovery*** 2019.
12. **DiNardo CD, … Konopleva M.** Molecular patterns of response and treatment failure after
    frontline venetoclax combinations in older AML. ***Blood*** 2020 (EGA EGAS00001003820).
