## What we are actually asking for (frame this precisely)
The decisive question is **not** "does ATAP kill leukemia cells" — prior ATAP work already
established cytotoxicity. The open, decision-relevant question is the **therapeutic window**:

> **Primary endpoint = selectivity ratio** =
> LD50 in BAX/BAK-deficient, venetoclax-resistant leukemia cells
> ÷ LD50 in normal CD34+ hematopoietic progenitors / PBMC controls.

A selectivity ratio ≪ 1 (kills resistant leukemia at doses that spare normal marrow) is the only
result that would justify further ATAP development for this indication. This is **one bounded
experiment**, not open-ended lab access — that is what makes it a reasonable cold ask.

## Pre-approval timeline (must be understood BEFORE any bench work)
1. **Mentor sign-off** — a supervising scientist/PI must agree to sponsor and oversee.
2. **Institutional review** — student/visitor bench work needs the host institution's Scientific
   Review Committee (SRC) and/or IRB determination; biosafety (IBC) registration for human cell
   lines. Typical lead time: **weeks to a few months** — plan around it, do not assume same-semester.
3. **Materials/IP** — ATAP peptide sourcing + any MTA; confirm the Rutgers ATAP patent scope with an
   IP professional before framing anything as proprietary (tracked in `docs/BLOCKERS.md`).
4. **Only after 1–3**: schedule the single selectivity experiment.
