## Verdict

**(a) Is the ATAP *hematologic indication* novel?** *Plausibly yes, on this screening search.* All
located ATAP therapeutic work is solid-tumor (prostate); no publication or trial applying ATAP /
ATAP-iRGD-M8 to leukemia/lymphoma/myeloma or to BH3-mimetic resistance was found. This is the
project's main candidate novelty — the *idea* of ATAP for BH3-mimetic-resistant blood cancer.
**Caveat (honest, load-bearing):** the project's own data (cycles 3–10) shows the executioner-loss
phenotype ATAP targets is **rare (~1–2%)** and **not identifiable as a general-resistance driver**
in any available cohort. So the stronger "predict-and-map which venetoclax-resistant patients are
ATAP-salvageable" framing is **undercut** — you cannot map a phenotype that public cohorts barely
contain. The defensible novel claim shrinks to: *proposing ATAP for the specific, rare acquired-
executioner-loss subset as a mechanistically-rational hypothesis for wet-lab test* — a hypothesis,
not a validated map. Novelty here is conceptual, contingent on a human confirming no prior heme
ATAP work exists in fuller databases (Google Scholar, patents, conference abstracts, ClinicalTrials).

**(b) Are the guardian-axis / biomarker results novel?** **No — confirmatory.** The cycle-8/9/10
findings (BCL2 and BCL2/MCL1 predict venetoclax sensitivity; monocytic signature predicts
resistance; static expression is a modest predictor) **match established literature closely** (Pei
monocytic resistance; BCL2/MCL1-ratio biomarkers; BH3 profiling > static expression). This is
reassuring for *correctness* — the pipeline recovers known biology — but it is **not a discovery**
and must never be presented as one. (Notably, BCL2A1 — the gene ATAP is derived from — is itself a
published monocytic/resistance marker.)

**(c) Patents.** ATAP composition patents are held by Rutgers. Scope re: hematologic indications is
**unknown from this search** and is a **question for a patent/IP professional** — this log does not
assess claim scope and is not legal advice.

## Bottom line for the project's honesty
- **Confirmatory (not novel):** all guardian-axis / venetoclax-biomarker results.
- **Candidate novelty (conceptual, human-verification-required):** the ATAP *heme indication* as a
  hypothesis for the rare executioner-loss subset — but the "map the salvage population" ambition is
  undercut by that subset's rarity in available data.
- Novelty may now be *cautiously* discussed in outputs, always with the two caveats above and the
  note that this is a screening search, not a systematic prior-art clearance.
