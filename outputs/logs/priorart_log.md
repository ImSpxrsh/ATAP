## Verdict (2026-07-10)

**(a) Literature novelty of the heme indication — appears OPEN.** ATAP / ATAP-iRGD-M8 is
a real, published Bax/Bak-independent mitochondrial pore-former (Bfl-1/BCL2A1 tail anchor,
aa ~147-175). All located therapeutic work is **solid-tumor** (prostate xenografts;
related tumor-penetrating pro-apoptotic iRGD fusions in gastric). The 2011 mechanistic
papers note in principle that ATAP can bypass Bcl-2-family-dysregulation resistance, but
**no located record** applies ATAP to a hematologic malignancy indication, to
venetoclax/BH3-mimetic resistance, or as a computationally-predicted executioner-loss
salvage. The specific contribution here — a computational susceptibility map coupling
executioner (BAX/BAK) loss to BH3-mimetic-class failure in blood cancers — is therefore
consistent with an open indication as of this dated search.

**(b) Patent gate — NOT cleared; flagged for a human.** ATAP composition/use patents are
held by Rutgers–RWJMS. Whether their claims extend to hematologic malignancies (or to
"any cancer") cannot be determined from secondary literature. **This is a search to
inform the gate, not legal advice.** A mentor / IP professional must pull the actual
patent family (Google Patents / USPTO / Espacenet) and read claim scope before any
novelty or freedom-to-operate assertion is made.
